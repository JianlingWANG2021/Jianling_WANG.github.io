About Me

I am a scientific computing engineer at the Institut Denis Poisson (CNRS), with extensive experience in numerical simulations, high-performance computing, deep learning, and astrophysics. My research focuses on large-scale data processing, machine learning applications, and advanced computational models.

Scientific Calculation

Expert in high-performance computing (HPC) with parallel computing using GPU/CUDA, MPI, and OpenMP. Developed and optimized numerical algorithms for large-scale astrophysical simulations.

Contributed to hydrodynamic simulations and non-linear instability studies, as well as deep learning applications in 3D reconstruction, medical imaging, and astrophysical data analysis.

Research

Worked on galaxy formation and evolution, observational data analysis, and theoretical modeling. Contributed to major telescope projects, including ELT-MOSAIC and LAMOST.

Developed computational models for large-scale simulations, including modifications to GADGET-2 and GIZMO for galaxy formation studies.

Publications

Author of 44 peer-reviewed publications, including 12 as first author. Review board member for several international journals.

Software & Tools

Developed multiple computational tools for astrophysical and data science research, including:

  • GizmoCox: Star formation and feedback module for GIZMO.
  • SGSST: 3D Gaussian Splatting for Neural Scene Stylization.
  • LAMBayMPI: Parallel Bayesian stellar parameter estimation.
  • BuildLFs: Galaxy luminosity function estimator.
